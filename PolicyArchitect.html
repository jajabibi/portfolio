Current Problem

Data breaches are in the news almost daily and cyber attackers are consistently reaching new levels of ambition and disruption. With such aggressive threats, companies need a more modern, efficient, and secure way to safeguard against breaches in order to protect their data centers and customers’ valuable information. But security administrators are struggling to understand the environments they need to protect. It is becoming increasingly challenging for them to create and manage security policies to combat threats effectively.

Final Results

After realizing much of the complexity and difficulty in data center and cloud security is perpetuated by limited data center visibility and complicated infrastructures, vArmour’s goal is to simplify the policy creation process.
Policy Architect aims to revolutionize the data center and cloud security space by providing increased visibility, simplified organization, and improved protection all through a flexible and intelligent way to create, manage, and validate intent-based policies that protect important applications and data.

Process

I have been involved in Policy Architect from the very beginning, starting from user research to final product release. The following graph is the overall process in a nutshell.

Exploratory Research

In order to improve current policy creation process, we had two approaches. One was from the UX perspective, we wanted to understand the existing state, and make the whole process simpler and smoother. The UX team had a chance to conduct remote user sessions and on-site visits with our customers and field engineers to gather information. The other perspective was from the data side. To create accurate and secure policies, data plays an important part. We collaborated with a data scientist to experiment with the actual traffic data.

Customer journey map was one of the deliverables that the UX team shared out about what we learned from current policy creation process. We also used it as a reference to brainstorm about future policy creation experience.

Design and Iterations

Wireframes

After workflow was finalized and confirmed with different teams (including PM and Engineering team), UX team started to create detail wireframes for each part of the product.

Asset Management, where users can view, organize and visualize their environment.
Policy Creation, where users can create security policies by using default template and data model.
Validation, where users can test the whole policy set by using historical date and prevent system downtime.

Prototyping Test

We created mid-fidelity prototypes and conducted several remote sessions with prospective customers. Because Policy Architect is a brand new product and doesn’t exist in the market yet, these prototyping sessions were very valuable in terms of validating some of our early concepts. We iterated designs based on what we learned from people who are hands on security admins.
